Caffeine and Coffee - a Love/Hate Relationship

  • The high caffeine content in some coffees can have adverse health impacts for drinkers.
  • Decaffeinating coffee is costly and negatively impacts taste.
  • With the right combination of beans, perhaps a low-caffeine blend can be acheived that preserves all the flavor complexity but minimizes the caffeine.

Coffee Data Set

App Structure

Since minimizing caffeine is the goal of the application, we use caffeine as the fixed Y variable in an interactive ploting application. This allows users to change the x variable to see how other components are distributed. The countries are presented by color, which allows us to look for trends that might aid us in selecting coffees for the blend.

Conclusion

  • By plotting this data, we can begin to see trends in the caffeine levels that might allow us to put together a blend while preserving important characteristics.
  • Clearly, arabica coffees contain a much lower level of caffeine but within arabica coffees, there is a lot of diversity in chemical composition.
  • Several components would warrent an in depth study. Trigonelline and chlorogenic acid are largely responsible for the bitterness in coffee. The fat content will laregely determine the appropriate production of crema in espresso. All of these factors will need to be balanced to create an ideal blend.
